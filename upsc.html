UPSC Civil Services

Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) Civil Services Examination is India's most prestigious competitive examination for IAS, IPS, IFS and other Central Government services.

Brief Description

UPSC Civil Services Examination is conducted annually by the Union Public Service Commission to recruit candidates for prestigious services such as IAS, IPS, IFS, IRS and other Group A and Group B government posts.

Exam Stages

  • Preliminary Examination
  • Main Examination
  • Personality Test (Interview)
  • Final Merit List

Subjects Covered

  • History
  • Geography
  • Indian Polity
  • Economics
  • Environment & Ecology
  • Science & Technology
  • Current Affairs
  • Ethics & Integrity
  • Essay Writing
  • Optional Subject

Career Opportunities

  • IAS Officer
  • IPS Officer
  • IFS Officer
  • IRS Officer
  • Indian Audit & Accounts Service
  • Indian Railway Management Service
  • Central Secretariat Service
  • Other Group A Services

Eligibility Criteria

  • Bachelor's Degree from a recognized university
  • Indian Citizen (for most services)
  • Minimum Age: 21 Years
  • Maximum Age: As per UPSC norms
  • Age Relaxation for Reserved Categories

Job Roles

  • District Collector
  • Superintendent of Police
  • Foreign Service Officer
  • Revenue Officer
  • Policy Maker
  • Administrative Officer
  • Diplomat
  • Government Administrator

Salary Structure

The starting salary of an IAS, IPS or IFS officer is approximately ₹56,100 per month (Level 10 Pay Matrix) along with allowances such as HRA, DA and TA. Senior officers earn significantly higher salaries.

Preparation Strategy

  • Read NCERT Books
  • Study Standard Reference Books
  • Read Daily Newspapers
  • Follow Current Affairs
  • Practice Answer Writing
  • Take Mock Tests
  • Revise Regularly
  • Develop Interview Skills

Skills Required

  • Leadership Skills
  • Decision Making
  • Communication Skills
  • Analytical Thinking
  • Problem Solving
  • Time Management
  • Public Administration Knowledge
  • Stress Management

Future Scope

UPSC-selected officers serve in key administrative, law enforcement, diplomatic and policymaking roles. It offers excellent career growth, job security, social impact and opportunities to contribute to nation-building.
